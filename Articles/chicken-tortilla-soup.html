Recipe

The bold flavors of this soup embody the vibrant flavors that we associate with Tex-Mex cuisine. When the tortilla chips soak in the chili-spiked broth, they soften, and the warm broth enhances the subtle corn flavor of the tortillas.

Vegetarians may use vegetable stock and omit the chicken.


Ingredients

Peppers

  • 1 medium red pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 medium green pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and chopped

    • Spices

  • 1 medium red onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (1 teaspoon)
  • 1/2 cup minced fresh cilantro
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano, or 1/8 cup fresh
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 3/4 teaspoon chili powder

    • Other

  • 1/8 cup canola oil
  • 1 cup diced canned tomatoes with juice
  • 4 cups cold water or chicken stock
  • 2/3 pound boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 (15-ounce) can of cooked black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels
  • Salt to taste
  • 8 ounces blue corn tortilla chips

    • Garnish (optional)

  • Diced ripe avocado
  • A sprinkling of grated Monterey Jack cheese

    • Directions

    Heat the canola oil in a sauce pot over medium-high heat. Sauté the red and green peppers, onion, garlic, oregano, cumin, and chili powder for 3 minutes, until the onion is translucent, stirring often. Add the jalapeno and tomatoes; continue stirring for 1 minute.

    Add the cold water and the whole chicken breasts. Bring the soup to a boil, reduce the heat, and simmer uncovered for 20 minutes. Remove the chicken breasts to cool, and cut them into bite-size pieces. Return the chicken to the soup, add the black beans and corn, and then return the soup to a boil. Remove the soup from the heat, stir in the cilantro, and season with the salt.

    To serve, slightly crunch a small handful of tortilla chips in the bottom of each bowl. Ladle the soup over the chips.

    To prepare the garnish, add the acovado and chees on top of the chips, then ladle the soup over the top, and serve.
